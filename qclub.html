Background

Overall context

QReward is a really fun customer loyalty app that gets you access to exclusive, relevant, local offers in your community. Each transaction rewards you with Q Bucks. You can also play games to earn QBucks. QReward provide solutions for business to increase customer retention. QReward is first launched in Vancouver, Canada.

My role

I worked as full-time Product Designer involving from UX research to UI design to the development handoff phase: stakeholder interview, user interview, research studies, prototyping, usability testing. I report my work to the founder on the daily basis and collaborate with development team outsourced in India.

Summary

The main problem when building this app is I need to bring in a lot of concepts into one single app: QReward is a combination of many concepts:

  • Customer loyalty
  • Gamification
  • E-commerce

In addition, as being the only designer in the company, I have to cover the entire UX/ UI process and the design department.

To solve this complexity, I apply a wide variety of methods in each phase:

  • Research: Surveys, in-depth virtual interviews, stakeholder interview, secondary study, competitor analysis. Stakeholder interview and competitor analysis are on my main focus because those two methods work well under low startup research budget.
  • Ideate: I use affinity mapping to group features into 3 main concepts as mentioned above.

Gamification

Customer loyalty & E-commerce

  • Prototype: Paper prototyping, low fi wireframes, high fidelity UI on Figma. I design UI components by modules. For example, I finalize the redemption flow, then use the same screens for redemption throughout the app to maintain consistency and save time.

Secondary research

  • A great loyalty app contains 2 elements: Transactional and Experiments. Experimental rewards will have long term impacts. Experimental rewards of QClub are Qingo/Gamification and Q Dreams.
  • Naturally, emotional design shouldn’t be restricted to transaction-based rewards.
  • The direction to develop QClub app: Progressive loyalty rewards & Emotional Design.
  • If you are looking for a mechanism that encapsulates the essence of progressive loyalty rewards, loyalty tiers are one of the most effective - Nielson Research.
  • A new study by Bond Brand Loyalty shows many of Canadian users are not spending their points. Right now, there are $16 billion worth of points left unredeemed by members in Canada.
  • 60% said program experience, brand alignment, digital experiences, and “human touch” were the biggest factors in their satisfaction.
  • What can keep users coming back every day: The hook model by Eyal. Clash Royale Game is a great example: Every time you play, you will get a chest, and you will open that chest because that’s your reward. However, you have to wait for a certain amount of time to open the chest (it depends on the type of chests that you get), and at some point, you will close the App and do your real-life activities. After the chest can be opened, it will send you a push notification to let you know that your chest can be opened now, you will go back to the App and open the chest.
