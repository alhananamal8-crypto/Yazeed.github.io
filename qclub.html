Background

Overall context

QClub is a really fun customer loyalty app that gives you access to exclusive, relevant, and local offers in your community. Shop and play games to earn QBucks. QClub App provide solutions for businesses to increase customer retention. The app will be launched early next year in BC, Canada.

My role

I worked as a full-time Product Designer doing everything from UX research to UI design to the development handoff phase. During the research phase, our team had 2 designers. One left when we started ideating: From that point on, I was the only designer in the team.

Problem statement

The main problem when building this app was the founder had strong concepts in his mind before I joined. When joining the team, my task was to bring many concepts into one single app: QClub app is a combination of many concepts:

  1. Customer loyalty
  1. Gamification
  1. E-commerce

Solution

To solve this complexity, I applied a wide variety of methods in each phase:

  • Research: Surveys, in-depth virtual interviews, empathy map, stakeholder interview, secondary study, affinity mapping, and competitor analysis. In-depth interview, secondary research and competitor analysis are my main focus because those methods work well under a low startup research budget.
  • Ideate: I use affinity mapping to group features into 3 main concepts as mentioned above.

Gamification

Customer loyalty & E-commerce

  • Prototype: Paper prototyping, low fi wireframes, and high fidelity UI on Figma. I design UI components by modules. For example, I finalize the redemption flow, then use the same screens for redemption throughout the app to maintain consistency and save time.

Research Process

In-depth Interviews

Why I chose this methodology:

We're a small startup with a small research budget. In-depth interviews allows us to use a smaller sample of participants compared to other research methodologies. I found all of the volunteer testers through the DesignX community and Seneca student community so I can save money on testing while being sure that they meet the key criteria: Using at least 1 reward app.

Number of participants: 4

Criteria: Using at least 1 reward app

Duration: 30-40 minutes per section

List of questions asked:

  1. Have you used any loyalty-rewards mobile applications before? (Describe) Tell us the story
  1. What kind of loyalty-rewards mobile app have you used before?
  1. How often did you use such app?
  1. The app you're using has the tier, how it works for them.
  1. What are your pain points when using customer loyalty apps?
  1. How do you typically redeem your reward points, in store or online? tell us more
  1. Which content do you like seeing from a loyalty customer app. Rank them
  1. Tell as about your most used loyalty program, what motivated you to join the program, and why is it your most used?

Why I chose this methodology:

The founder had strong detailed concepts about the app. He strongly believes in his initial sketches and didn't want to spend money on research. Secondary research

Why I chose this methodology: We're a small startup with a small research budget. Secondary research can save us money. In addition, customer loyalty is a field with a lot of great secondary research. Thanks to secondary research, I am able to convince the founder to change the concept of "Points" into "QBucks" as well as apply the hook model to QGames.

  • A great loyalty app contains 2 elements: Transactional and Experiments. Experimental rewards will have long term impacts. Experimental rewards of QClub are Qingo/Gamification and Q Dreams.
  • Naturally, emotional design shouldn’t be restricted to transaction-based rewards.
  • The direction to develop QClub app: Progressive loyalty rewards & Emotional Design.
  • If you are looking for a mechanism that encapsulates the essence of progressive loyalty rewards, loyalty tiers are one of the most effective - Nielson Research.
  • A new study by Bond Brand Loyalty shows many of Canadian users are not spending their points. Right now, there are $16 billion worth of points left unredeemed by members in Canada.
  • 60% said program experience, brand alignment, digital experiences, and “human touch” were the biggest factors in their satisfaction.
  • What can keep users coming back every day: The hook model by Eyal. Clash Royale Game is a great example: Every time you play, you will get a chest, and you will open that chest because that’s your reward. However, you have to wait for a certain amount of time to open the chest (it depends on the type of chests that you get), and at some point, you will close the App and do your real-life activities. After the chest can be opened, it will send you a push notification to let you know that your chest can be opened now, you will go back to the App and open the chest.
