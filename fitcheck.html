Fit Check

  1. UX Design
  2. UI Design
Context

Inspired by Fitbit, I creat "Fit Check" mobile app. My app can pull data from other health apps, such as iHealth, SamSung health, to track calories and generate personalized recipes for users.

My role

UX design and UI design. I started from the first stage of brainstorming to testing. Kindmond, Director at Smokin’ Dogs guided me through the process of choosing color theme and logo design for Fitcheck.

Type &
Duration

Type: Project
Duration: 3 months

Problem Statement ⚓

People of all ages have difficulty controlling the amount of calories they take in daily and receiving personalized diet based on their calories input. They have 2 needs.

Many health apps track the calories from the daily activites and food, but not generating personalized diets. While food apps, like Tasty, has a lot of cooking recipes but not calory tracking. FitCheck solves 2 user needs in one single app.

Design Process

01.

Empathize with users

I use 4 research methods: competitor analysis, heuristics analysis, 1-on-1 Interview, survey.

02.

Define problem

Many millennial users lose control of the calories they eat everyday. They need one single app that can track calory input and generate personalized recipes based on the data

03.

Ideate

Hand sketch wireframes, high fidelity prototype, UI design and mockups.

04.

Test and Final results

The test participants, millennials and housewives, say the app is so convenient and beatifully designed!

Empathize with Users 👟

Competitor Analysis

Heuristics Analysis

I identified Tasty, Yummly and All Recipe Diner Spinner as the competitors. I created heuristics analysis among three of them

Survey

I prepared a list of interview and survey questions to ask 10 participants about their cooking habit as well as their nutrition concern. I interviewed participants face to face, on phone and online. View the online interview form here 👈

  1. 40% always cook at home while 40% never/rarely cook at home
  2. Dinner is the most important meal with 70%
  3. 50% doesn't follow any diet
  4. The most popular reason for not trying an food app is because they prefer Google and Youtube search

Ideate Solution 💡

FitCheck can pull data from other health apps, such as iHealth, Samsung Health, FitBit. After processing these data, FitCheck produces a personalized diet for the users. The app also keeps tracking if the user is following the diet.
