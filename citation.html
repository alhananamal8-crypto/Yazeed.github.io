EasyCitation

  1. Product Design
  2. UX Design
Context

"Easy Citation" is originally my submission at Adobe Jam Contest 2019. The topic is to create a mobile app that encourage academic integrity. Within only 3 hours, My teammate and I came to the idea of creating a new app to generate citations.

My role

After the Adobe Jam contest, I decided to revamp the entire project, from Product design, UX research to UI visual design.

Type &
Duration

Type: Project for Contest
Duration: 1st round: 4 hours.
2nd round: 3 months

Problem Statement

Putting citations in the right format is a nightmare for students. Manually create citations into proper formats like APA, MLA, Chicago is tough because there so many academic rules to follow and tons of details you need to include.

What’s worse? There is no mobile app to generate citations in the market yet. Citation Machine and Turnitin are big names for academic integrity; however, they only support website format, not as an app or a chrome extension

Design Process

I went through 4 steps in my design process.

01.

Empathize with users

We used 3 research methods: 1-on-1 interview, Cardsorting, Competitor analysis

02.

Define problem

Many students do not know how to write citations/references following a specific style like APA, MLA or Chicago. Even if they know how to, they do not store their references in one neat place.

03.

Ideate

Sitemap, Taskflow, Userflow, UI design

04.

Test and Final results

I bring the app testing around in my class and presented to my profesors. A pitch deck for "Easy Citation" is ready!

Design Process

Empathize with Users

1-on-1 Interview
I prepared a list of interview questions to talk to 6 participants about their sharing academic integrity issues. I interviewed all participants face to face.

    Concrete, innocent questions that are easy to answer:

  • Do you think citation is important? Why or Why not?
  • What kind of information (e.g. photo, text) that you put citation to?
  • What is your pain point?

    • Questions that cover the main purpose

  • What is the thing you hate the most about creating citation?
  • Are you familiar with different citation styles? Which style you hear about? Which style you actually know how to create it?
  • Which tool you use to tackle plagiarism?
  • Who and where you gonna seek help? Why?
  • Which device/tool you use when you want to learn more about plagiarism? Which source/site?
  • If there is an app/ website/ chrome extension about plagiarism, what do you expect?
  • What can make you try a new-launched citation app?

    • Round off and thanks:

  • Do you want to add anything that we have not included?

Open cardsoting
I prepared about 40 cards with different keywords on it. The same 6 participants were asked to group those cards and give a name to each group.

Design Process

Define problem

Firstly, many students are used to the habbit of asking their friends when they want to create citations. Other students generate citations on websites. To make EasyCitation become popular, I have to educate users the habbit of using a smart phone to create citations.

Secondly, most people work on their academic papers on laptop and computer; hence it is more convenient to generate citations on websites like CitationMachine.net. To solve this, I create functions like “Copy text”, “Save to library” and “Sync” so that users can still upload/sync citations on the computer after generating them on mobile app. A Chrome extension of EasyCitation will be introduced.
